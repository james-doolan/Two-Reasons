Two Reasons

Introduction

40 million people died in WW1 and 60 millon in WW2. Around two thirds of whom were civilians in the case of the latter.

The two reaons another world war should never take place could simply be given as the two atomic bombs which were dropped over Japan. However, events of recent years have shown an appetite amongst demagogues for conflict and the potential for global unrest seems to be nausiatingly high. Despite the consequences of such bombs being detonated, some leaders of nations which possess them still brandish them boisterously.

WW2

6 million Jewish people were murdered in the second world war.

University students today are less than a third as likely to study history compared to those in the middle of the last century. This regression in appreciation of these historical events could be playing a role in peoples current ideological leanings. During the creation of this website I became much more sickened than I had before by the site of a swastika. Incredibly, it is still being graffitied on walls. This led me to question using Nazi Germany's flag, which includes the swastika, but I felt it necessary for it to be presented in context of the evil which it represents. Studying that period of history brought those events much closer to home and I hope to do the same for the people that use this website to study the wars.
